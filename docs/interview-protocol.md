# The Interview Protocol

Ask these **one at a time**, conversationally. After each answer, reflect back what you heard (this catches misunderstandings early) and follow up on anything surprising before moving on. At the end, distill everything into `PROFILE.md` using `templates/PROFILE.template.md`.

## The 10 questions

1. **Current situation** — How would you describe your role/situation right now? Not the title — what do you actually spend your time on?
2. **Vision (2–3 years)** — If things go *well*, what does your life look like? Work, income, location, relationships. Ask for the daydream, not the safe answer.
3. **Strengths** — What are you genuinely good at? What have you pulled off recently that surprised you? (Capture the shadow side of each strength too.)
4. **Internal blockers** — What does distraction/derailment actually look like on a bad day? What has previously knocked you off track, and what got you back?
5. **External blockers** — What's practically in the way of the next milestone: money, credentials, portfolio, connections, time, location?
6. **The gap** — What proof/assets do you have right now toward your goal, and what's the single most impressive thing you could build in the near term to close the gap?
7. **Trusted people** — Who do you trust for advice, in which domains (money, business, relationships, faith, tech)? Where is there *no one*? (Empty seats tell you what the board must cover.)
8. **Decision style** — How do you actually make decisions (especially money)? One you got right, one you got wrong. What would advice have to look like for you to trust and *follow* it? Push vs. gentle?
9. **Next 90 days** — What 2–3 concrete things must happen to be "on track"? Force measurable success criteria.
10. **Warning signs & non-negotiables** — (a) The *early* signals you're starting to spiral, so your coach can call them out. (b) What you'll never compromise, no matter the goal.

## Why one at a time

Batch questionnaires get resume answers. A conversation gets the truth — the follow-up after "I'm unemployed" is where the actual profile lives (side income, opening dates, who they're trying to become). Budget ~20–30 minutes.

## After the interview

1. Write `PROFILE.md` (from the template) — include a **"How to coach me"** section translating their answers into explicit instructions.
2. Recommend 3 advisors whose expertise maps to the **empty seats** found in Q7 — not generic famous people.
3. Save a pointer in Claude's persistent memory so every future session loads the profile before giving advice.
